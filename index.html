The Origin

Masu

THE SACRED VESSEL OF JAPAN

1,300 Years of Meaning

For over thirteen centuries, the masu has held a sacred place in Japanese life. Originally a measure for rice, it evolved into a vessel for ceremony, celebration, and prayer.

KUMIKI transforms this ancient form into the world's smallest masu — a piece of wearable art, just two centimeters across, carved from hinoki cypress and adorned with precious metals and traditional Japanese glass.

Each piece is sealed with a glass coating that preserves the natural grain of the wood forever.

Sustainability

The 1,300-Year Circle

The hinoki cypress used in our masu is harvested from sustainably managed forests in Japan's heartland.

For every tree used, new saplings are planted — continuing a cycle of cultivation that has endured for over a millennium. A portion of each purchase supports forestry education programs in rural Japan.

When you hold a KUMIKI piece, you hold a fragment of a living forest.
